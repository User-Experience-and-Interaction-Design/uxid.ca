Instructions for Prospective Graduate Students

Professor Ali Neshati, UXID Lab

Before You Email Professor Neshati

Thank you for your interest in pursuing graduate studies under the supervision of Professor Ali Neshati. Please carefully read the following instructions before emailing your inquiry:

  1. Research the Lab's Work:

    Before reaching out, ensure you are familiar with Professor Neshati's research areas. Review his publications, ongoing projects, and the scope of the research group. You can find his profile and publications on Google Scholar. Tailor your email to show how your research interests align with the lab's work.

  2. Prepare Your Documents:

    Attach your CV, academic transcript, and a brief statement of research interests. These documents are essential for assessing your background and potential fit with the lab. Make sure the files are clearly labeled (e.g., "YourName_CV.pdf").

  3. Keep the Email Concise:

    In the email body, briefly introduce yourself, mention your educational background, and explain why you are interested in working with Professor Neshati. Highlight relevant experience and skills, such as research experience, publications, or technical expertise. Be respectful and professional in your tone.

  4. Explain Your Motivation:

    In addition to discussing your research interests, explain why you are specifically interested in Professor Neshati’s lab. Mention any relevant projects, papers, or ideas you have that would align with his research directions.

  5. Check Funding Availability:

    If you require funding, inquire about available funding opportunities in the email. Mention any external scholarships or awards you have applied for or are planning to apply for.

  6. Be Patient:

    Professors receive a high volume of emails from prospective students. If you don’t receive an immediate response, please be patient. If after two to three weeks you have not received a response, you may send a polite follow-up email.

Example Email Format

Below is an example of how to structure your email:

Dear Professor Neshati,

My name is [Your Name], and I recently graduated with a [Your Degree] in [Your Major] from [Your University]. I am highly interested in pursuing a [Master's/Ph.D.] in [Your Research Area] under your supervision.

I have reviewed your publications and am particularly interested in your work on [specific topic]. I believe my research experience in [related area] would be a good fit for your lab. Attached are my CV, transcript, and a brief statement of my research interests.

I would greatly appreciate the opportunity to discuss potential research opportunities in your lab. Thank you for your time, and I look forward to hearing from you.

Sincerely,  
[Your Name]  
[Your Contact Information]

For any questions, feel free to email ali.neshati@ontariotechu.ca.
